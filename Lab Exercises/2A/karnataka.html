Special Features of Kerala

Culture and Heritage

  • Traditional Arts: Kerala is famous for its classical dance forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, and traditional martial art Kalaripayattu.
  • Festivals: Onam, Vishu, and Thrissur Pooram are celebrated with grandeur and enthusiasm.
  • Architecture: The state is known for its unique style of architecture seen in temples, churches, and mosques.

Natural Beauty

  • Backwaters: The backwaters of Kerala, particularly in Alleppey and Kumarakom, offer serene and picturesque experiences.
  • Hill Stations: Munnar, Wayanad, and Thekkady are famous for their scenic beauty and pleasant climate.
  • Beaches: Kovalam, Varkala, and Marari are popular for their beautiful beaches and tranquil environment.

Gastronomy

  • Traditional Cuisine: Kerala's cuisine is known for its rich flavors, especially seafood, coconut, and spices. Must-try dishes include appam with stew, puttu, and fish curry.
  • Sadya: A traditional vegetarian feast served on a banana leaf, usually during festivals and special occasions.
  • Snacks: Banana chips, pazhampori, and unniyappam are popular snacks enjoyed by locals and visitors alike.

Economy and Industries

  • Tourism: Kerala is a major tourist destination, known as "God's Own Country" for its natural beauty and cultural heritage.
  • Spices and Agriculture: The state is a leading producer of spices like black pepper, cardamom, and cloves, as well as rubber and coconuts.
  • IT Industry: Cities like Kochi and Thiruvananthapuram are emerging as important hubs for information technology and software services.
